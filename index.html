SUSTAINABILITY THROUGH THE AGES

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

Intel's Sustainability Timeline

1968

Intel Founded

Intel Corporation is founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, marking the beginning of decades of innovation.

Fun Fact: The name "Intel" comes from "Integrated Electronics"!
1971

First Microprocessor

Intel introduces the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, launching the microprocessor revolution.

Fun Fact: The 4004 had just 2,300 transistorsâ€”modern chips have billions!
1978

8086 Processor

Intel launches the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that powers countless computers today.

Fun Fact: The x86 architecture is still used in most PCs today!
1985

386 Microprocessor

Intel introduces the 386 microprocessor, bringing 32-bit computing and multitasking to personal computers.

Fun Fact: The 386 was the first Intel chip to allow running multiple programs at once!
2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations.

Fun Fact: Intel reduced its GGE by more than 75% from its 2006 peak through investments in renewable energy and efficiency!
2020

RISE strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy and 2030 goals, aiming to drive industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

Fun Fact: RISE stands for Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling!
2022

100% Renewable Energy

Intel achieved 100% renewable electricity use in its global operations.

Fun Fact: After 2006, Intel invested in renewable energy and efficiency, reducing its greenhouse gas emissions by more than 75%!
Fun Fact: Intel is one of the largest voluntary purchasers of green power in the U.S.!
2030

Net Positive Water

Intel aims to achieve net positive water use by conserving more water than it consumes.

Fun Fact: Intel restores billions of gallons of water to local watersheds each year!
2040

Net Zero Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Intel is committed to reaching net zero greenhouse gas emissions in its global operations.

Fun Fact: Intel aims to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040!

RISE Strategy

Under its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy, Intel sets ambitious 2030 goals, including driving industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

Commitment

In 2022, Intel pledged to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) by 2040. This commitment builds on decades of environmental initiatives and partnerships across the semiconductor industry.

Water & Waste

Intel conserves billions of gallons of water annually and collaborates with local communities to restore watersheds. Intel also upcycles and recycles materials to reduce waste and advance a circular economy.
